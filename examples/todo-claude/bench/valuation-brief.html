Quality — code and artifacts

Code roughly a tie

Both build, both pass the Tags tests. Companion's code is more correct (its data model can't orphan a tag on todo-deletion; spec-kit's leaks one). Spec-kit's is better crafted (cleaner components, tidier tests). The twist: Companion's model also introduced a crash-on-upgrade bug that its review then had to patch. Robustness by review, not by design.

Artifacts split decision

Better to build from: Companion (wave tasks + the review caught a real bug). Better to read & maintain: spec-kit (its research.md drove the cleaner architecture; its prose is readable). Companion's docs are denser and break the repo's own writing rules. Your screenshot, confirmed.

What it adds up to

The two analyses converged: Companion traded an up-front design step (research) for a later defensive fix (review). Same total time, but it's robustness-by-patch instead of by-design, and the docs read worse. The adversarial review is a keeper (cheap, caught a real bug). The parallelism, as built, isn't earning its complexity.

The decision

Stop diverging. Rebuild Companion to faithfully mimic stock spec-kit (the readable shape, the research step), and add back only what's needed or proven: size-based fast-path, correct timing capture, and auto-complete at the end. Hold parallel and adversarial as layers to re-add and measure later. That faithful baseline is what we build next, and what we A/B against stock, cutting or replacing from there.
